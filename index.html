Rooted Cultural Roots. Global Wellness.

Wellness has roots. Fitness should honor them.

Global fitness platforms treat the world as one market: the same routines, the same gear, the same generic "wellness content" from Mumbai to Osaka. Rooted starts from the opposite premise — every country already has centuries of wellness wisdom, and the most sustainable fitness product is the one that revives it instead of replacing it.

Rooted pairs each user's workouts with the wellness heritage of their region, scores the environmental footprint of how they train with a proprietary Sustainability Quotient (SQ), and points people describing an everyday health concern toward a culturally-rooted practice worth trying — backed by an in-house Cultural Practice Library, not marketing copy.

Cultural Wellness Engine

Matches every workout to a local practice — Ayurvedic recovery in India, temazcal-inspired breathwork in Mexico, shinrin-yoku forest walks in Japan, Pura Vida gratitude rituals in Costa Rica.

Sustainability Quotient

A transparent, weighted score (0–100) built from gear choice, travel mode, and post-workout resource use — not a black box, a formula students and judges can audit line by line.

Wellness Advisor

Describe an everyday health concern — stress, poor sleep, low energy, aches — and get a culturally-rooted practice to try from each region, plus clear guidance on when to see a real professional instead.

Community Wellness Hub

An in-house Cultural Practice Library plus Community Connect — create or join a group with people training toward the same practice, city or goal. No outside organizations involved; everything here is Rooted's own content and your own community.

Wellness Advisor

Describe an everyday health concern in your own words. The advisor matches it against a small knowledge base of common, non-emergency wellness concerns and suggests a culturally-rooted practice from each region worth trying.

Not medical advice. This tool offers general wellness suggestions only — it cannot diagnose or treat any condition. For persistent, severe or worsening symptoms, or any emergency, contact a licensed healthcare professional or local emergency services.
